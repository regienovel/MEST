M-PESA FRAUD SYSTEM FREEZES THOUSANDS OF ACCOUNTS DURING HOLIDAY RUSH
The Standard (Nairobi) — December 28, 2023

Thousands of small business owners across Kenya reported their M-Pesa accounts were frozen without warning during the busy Christmas trading period, in what appears to be a mass false-positive event triggered by Safaricom's automated fraud detection system.

The disruption hit hardest in Nairobi's Gikomba market, Mombasa's Old Town, and Kisumu's Kibuye market, where mama mbogas (vegetable sellers), boda boda drivers, and small-scale traders rely on M-Pesa as their primary — and often only — means of receiving payment.

"I had customers standing in front of me wanting to pay, and my account just said 'suspended,'" said Mary Wanjiku, a vegetable seller in Gikomba who estimates she lost KES 15,000 (approximately $100) in sales over two days. "No message, no explanation. I called Safaricom and waited forty minutes. They told me it was 'under review.'"

Safaricom confirmed in a brief statement that its "fraud prevention systems flagged an elevated number of transactions for review during the holiday period" but insisted the system was "working as designed to protect customers."

Mobile money analysts say the issue likely stems from how the fraud detection model defines "normal" transaction patterns. During the December holiday season, informal traders experience a dramatic surge in both transaction volume and individual transaction sizes as Kenyans purchase food, gifts, and travel. For many small traders, December volumes can be 3-5 times their monthly average.

"The model sees a mama mboga who normally processes 20 transactions a day suddenly doing 80, and it flags that as suspicious," explained Dr. Tonny Omwansa, a mobile money researcher at the University of Nairobi. "But that's completely normal seasonal behaviour. The model was trained on average patterns and doesn't understand that December is different."

The Communications Authority of Kenya said it received over 2,000 complaints related to M-Pesa account freezes in the final week of December, compared to a typical weekly average of approximately 150.

Several affected traders reported that their accounts remained frozen for 48-72 hours, with no clear process for expedited review. Some said they were told to visit a Safaricom shop with their national ID to have the freeze lifted, but shops were overcrowded and understaffed during the holidays.

The Kenya National Chamber of Commerce and Industry called on Safaricom to implement seasonal adjustments to its fraud detection thresholds and to provide immediate notification and a rapid appeal process when accounts are frozen.

Safaricom processes approximately 61 million M-Pesa transactions daily, and the platform handles roughly 50% of Kenya's GDP. For many informal economy workers, even a single day without M-Pesa access can mean the difference between eating and going hungry.
